Computer Science Unit 2 Questions

Question: Show understanding of the purpose and benefits of networking devices
Answer: Networking devices facilitate communication between computers, enhancing connectivity and efficiency.
Question: Show understanding of the characteristics of a LAN and a WAN
Answer: LAN is local, WAN spans larger distances; differences include speed and coverage.
Question: Explain the client-server and peer-to-peer models of networked computers
Answer: Client-server involves centralized resources, peer-to-peer shares resources among connected devices.
Question: Show understanding of thin-client and thick-client and the differences between them
Answer: Thin-client relies on a server for processing, while thick-client handles processing locally.
Question: Show understanding of the bus, star, mesh, and hybrid topologies
Answer: Bus, star, mesh, and hybrid are network topologies with distinct structures and advantages.
Question: Understand how packets are transmitted between two hosts for a given topology
Answer: Packet transmission involves data division and routing for efficient communication.
Question: Show understanding of cloud computing, including the use of public and private clouds
Answer: Cloud computing involves accessing and storing data over the internet; public and private clouds differ in shared vs. dedicated usage.
Question: Show understanding of the differences between and implications of the use of wireless and wired networks
Answer: Wireless offers mobility, wired provides stability; considerations include speed, security, and deployment.
Question: Describe the characteristics of copper cable, fibre-optic cable, radio waves, microwaves, satellites
Answer: Different cables and waves vary in speed, signal type, and application within a network.
Question: Describe the hardware that is used to support a LAN
Answer: LAN hardware includes switches, servers, NICs, WNICs, WAPs, cables, bridges, and repeaters.
Question: Describe the role and function of a router in a network
Answer: Routers direct data traffic between networks, ensuring efficient communication.
