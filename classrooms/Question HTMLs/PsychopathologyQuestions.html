Psychopathology Questions:

Question: What does the statistical infrequency definition of abnormality imply?
Answer: The statistical infrequency definition suggests that a disorder is abnormal if its frequency is more than two standard deviations away from the mean incidence rates on a normally-distributed bell curve.
Question: How is statistical infrequency used in clinical diagnoses, and what are its limitations?
Answer: Statistical infrequency is used in clinical diagnoses for comparison with a baseline or 'normal' value, assessing the disorder's severity. However, it assumes that any abnormal characteristics are automatically negative, which is not always the case.
Question: According to the failure to function adequately definition, when is a person considered abnormal?
Answer: The failure to function adequately definition suggests that if a person's current mental state prevents them from leading a 'normal' life, they may be considered abnormal. This includes not obeying social and interpersonal rules, distress, and dangerous behavior.
Question: What is a major strength of the failure to function adequately definition?
Answer: A major strength is that it takes into account the patient's perspective, incorporating both subjective self-reported symptoms and the psychiatrist's objective opinion for more accurate diagnoses.
Question: What is a major weakness of the failure to function adequately definition?
Answer: A major weakness is the potential labeling of patients as 'strange' or 'crazy,' contributing to negative stereotypes about mental health disorders and possible discrimination or prejudice.
Question: According to the deviation from social norms definition, what determines 'abnormal' behavior?
Answer: The deviation from social norms definition suggests that 'abnormal' behavior is based on straying away from the social norms specific to a certain culture.
Question: What historical examples highlight discrimination related to the deviation from social norms?
Answer: In the nineteenth century, the diagnosis of 'nymphomania' was used to control women's behavior, reflecting discrimination and social control.
Question: What is a drawback of the deviation from ideal mental health definition?
Answer: The main issue is that the definition may have unrealistic expectations, potentially considering the majority of the population abnormal, leading to limited diagnostic accuracy.
Question: How does the deviation from ideal mental health definition suffer from cultural relativism?
Answer: The concept of self-actualization may be viewed differently in collectivist and individualist cultures, suggesting that the definition might only be accepted in certain cultural contexts.
